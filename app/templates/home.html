Our Process



Step 1: Finding a Dataset and Preprocessing it

Our main dataset is the 2018 Electronics reviews from Kaggle, which contains reviews about electronics from Amazon from 2018 and earlier. The data is about 9,000 reviews from ⤻Amazon in 2018. We also have supplementary reviews that we webscraped for the positive and negative models (our extension idea). The positive model was trained on 7000+ reviews while the negative one was trained on 5800+. We preprocessed them by cleaning them and saved the cleaned version of the data to train our models.



Step 2: Training the Model

After importing our cleaned dataset of reviews on Amazon electronics, we adjusted it to work on our model and ran the training. We didn't work with the positive or negative data yet because we needed to make sure our model would work. We experimented with the hyperparameters by recording reviews and comparing which ones with the different parameters made the most sense. Once we had chosen our models with the right hyperparameters, it was time to begin on our extension idea.



Step 3: Extending the Idea and Sentiment Analysis

Using our initial dataset, we ran sentiment analysis on the data and came up with two separate datasets, one for positive reviews and one for negative reviews. We trained two models with each of the datasets to make our final product take in the prompt, and return two separate reviews: one positive and one negative.



Step 4: Making the Website

After everything was done, we had to work on showcasing it. We worked together on the website, the front end engineers working on the layout and content of what you actually see, and the back end engineers connecting the model to the actual website, allowing us to actually try it out!


Difficulties

Use your mouse to drag to the right to see more!

Training the Model

At first, when we trained the model, we were getting outputs that made no sense. We had to be patient and experiment with the parameters to get results that made sense. Training the model also took a lot of time, so we had to be patient for that as well.

Debugging

Coding, especially larger projects like this, often leads to errors before getting a working product. Finding whats wrong with each and every error and having to google ones we didn't understand was very tedious and slowed us down.

Sentiment Analysis

Running the sentiment analysis was crucial in creating our model's positive and negative reviews, but running it took a very long time. We had to patient and debug a lot of it to finally get it to work the way we wanted to.

Retraining the models

We had already created one model that generated solid reviews, but we were determined to extend our idea. We had to retrain two models to create the positive and negative reviews, so that process, similar to our first time training the model, was time-consuming and challenging.
